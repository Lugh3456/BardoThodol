Bardo Thodol

The Tibetan Book of the Dead

Tibetan Buddhism · Nyingma Tradition

The Bardo Thodol (Tibetan: "Liberation Through Hearing During the Intermediate State"), known in the West as the Tibetan Book of the Dead, is a Nyingma terma text traditionally attributed to the teachings of Padmasambhava and revealed by Karma Lingpa in the fourteenth century. It is a guide recited for the dying, the recently deceased, and their living relatives, describing the journey through the "bardo" — the intermediate state between death and rebirth — in three stages: the Chikhai Bardo, the Chonyid Bardo, and the Sidpa Bardo, each presenting both an opportunity for liberation and possible pitfalls. This site follows that three-stage structure, presenting the text section by section with line-by-line commentary and a modern perspective for study and reflection.
